This Week

WotC's 2026 roadmap arrives — and raises as many questions as it answers

Confirmed
2026 D&D roadmap unveiled at GAMA — three seasons, four books, revived organised play
At GAMA Expo (early March 2026), WotC confirmed the full 2026 publishing framework: Season of Horror (Apr–Jun) anchored by Ravenloft: The Horrors Within, Season of Magic (Jul–Sep) anchored by Arcana Unleashed and Deadfall, Season of Champions (Oct–Dec) with anchor product TBA. Each season ties a major book to accessories and organised play.
The Seasons model is a structural shift — WotC is moving from standalone book releases to coordinated product suites. The accessory attach rate will determine whether this model succeeds commercially.
Founder's Read

The Seasons model creates predictable cultural windows — horror in Q2, magic in Q3, unknown in Q4. Each window is a content opportunity for third parties. The Season of Horror window opens April 13 with pre-orders. If your team can ship into it, the timing argument is now.

Variety — GAMA coverage · EN World — roadmap analysis
Confirmed
Ravenloft: The Horrors Within — June 16 release, pre-orders April 13, priced at $59.99
WotC's first 2026 anchor book includes horror-themed subclasses, species, backgrounds, Dark Gift feats, Darklords, and Fearsome Mists Denizens. Supporting accessories — Tarokka Deck, DM Screen, Map Pack — all $24.99. D&D Beyond tiered access: Master Tier June 2, Hero Tier June 9, wide release June 16.
Full-attach spend per customer is $134.96 (book + all three accessories). This is the revenue thesis for the Seasons model — WotC is testing whether community enthusiasm converts to accessory purchases, not just the core book.
Founder's Read

WotC's accessory line is atmospheric, not procedural — a Tarokka Deck and a DM Screen don't tell DMs how to run horror. That gap is the opportunity. A focused horror DM toolkit (fear mechanics, tension pacing, gothic encounter tables) priced $15–20 would land alongside Ravenloft without competing with it.

TTRPG Fans — roadmap detail · DnD Fan — release schedule
Confirmed
WotC launches Winter 2026 community survey — no NDA, public discussion permitted
WotC launched a public community survey covering game satisfaction, product preferences, trust signals, and D&D Beyond usage. Unlike previous design surveys, this one carries no NDA — community discussion of responses is permitted and actively happening on EN World and D&D Beyond forums.
The survey's transparency is a deliberate trust signal — and creates an obligation. Communities interpret public surveys as commitments to act on feedback. Silence after publishing results will be read as performative.
Founder's Read

Community discussion of the survey is a free research window. Threads on EN World and D&D Beyond are revealing what players actually want vs. what WotC is providing. Any gap between stated needs and the 2026 slate is a positioning opportunity — read them as primary market research.

D&D Beyond — survey page · EN World — survey discussion
Confirmed
D&D Beyond publishes platform roadmap — Character Builder 2.0 listed, no delivery date
D&D Beyond published a public platform roadmap for 2026: Event Finder for organised play, mobile character sheet improvements, and Character Builder 2.0 — a ground-up rebuild for the 2024 rules. No delivery date was given for Builder 2.0.
A public roadmap creates accountability. Community frustration with the 2014/2024 split in the current builder is documented. Any delay will be a significant trust event.
Founder's Read

Until Character Builder 2.0 ships, there is a gap in 2024-native digital tooling. Third-party character tools, 2024 rules quick-reference guides, or onboarding materials that bridge the split have structural demand that won't close until WotC delivers — which may be months away.

DnD Fan — platform updates · EN World — roadmap thread
Confirmed
Mythozoology Kickstarter launches — DnD Shorts × Loot Tavern, "100% human-made"
Heliana & Ryoko's Guide to Mythozoology — a D&D 5e creature and crafting sourcebook from DnD Shorts and Loot Tavern — launched on Kickstarter this week, closing April 16. The campaign prominently labels itself "100% human-made, guaranteed."
Creator IP collaboration campaigns with cross-pollinated audiences are outperforming solo launches structurally. The "human-made" label is being used as a purchase differentiator, not just an ethical disclosure.
Founder's Read

Day-one Kickstarter performance is almost entirely determined by combined mailing lists going in. The most valuable pre-production investment for a crowdfunding campaign is identifying a creator with a complementary audience — not refining the campaign page. Watch the close on April 16 for the final 2026 benchmark number.

EN World — crowdfunding roundup
Speculative
Season of Champions anchor product unknown — community speculation points to Dark Sun
WotC has confirmed the Season of Champions (Oct–Dec) exists but has not named its anchor. UA playtests for a Psion class, Gladiator Fighter, and Circle of Preservation Druid suggest a Dark Sun direction. No primary source confirms this.
Dark Sun would be the biggest D&D story of 2026. It has almost no modern 5e-compatible third-party material and a passionate existing fanbase.
Founder's Read

Watch for a D&D Direct or convention announcement in the May–July window. If you're considering Dark Sun-adjacent content (post-apocalyptic fantasy, survival mechanics, psionic-flavoured options), develop the themes without protected IP now so you can ship quickly when the announcement drops.

This Week's Action

What this week tells your team — and what to do about it

For your content and products

The most valuable signal from the roadmap reveal isn't Ravenloft — it's the Seasons structure. WotC has handed third-party creators a predictable cultural calendar: horror in Q2, magic in Q3, mystery in Q4. You now know which windows exist and when community attention concentrates. For Horror: April 13 is when pre-orders open and search intent begins building. June 16 is peak. If your team can ship into that window, you benefit from organic discovery lift that doesn't exist outside it.

The horror DM tooling gap is the clearest underserved space from this week's data. WotC's accessory line is atmospheric, not procedural. A focused product — fear mechanics, tension pacing, gothic encounter generation — positioned as DM craft, not setting lore, sits in a real gap with no direct WotC competition.

The "human-made" positioning signal from Mythozoology is worth acting on immediately. Audiences are actively using it as a purchase filter. Lead with it, make your authorship process visible, and treat it as a differentiator — not just disclosure. It costs nothing and is working in the market right now.

For your DM practice

The Season of Horror content window starting April 13 is also a research window. The community will produce significant volume of Ravenloft prep content, horror campaign guides, and subclass breakdowns between now and June 16. Read it as primary research into what DMs actually find difficult about running horror — that's market intelligence, not just inspiration.

The D&D Beyond Character Builder limitations are real and ongoing. Know the 2014/2024 workarounds and consider whether any of your own tools could help your group's table.

As players

Ravenloft pre-orders open April 13. The book covers multiple horror subgenres with new subclasses, species, backgrounds, and Dark Gift feats — more mechanically rich than Van Richten's Guide. If you're running or planning a horror campaign, June 16 is the date to plan around.
