WIZARDS OF THE COAST // OFFICIAL ANNOUNCEMENT

The Mists Are Rising: Season of Horror Officially Launches This Month

Wizards of the Coast's new "Seasons" publishing model kicks into gear — and pre-orders for Ravenloft: The Horrors Within open in just nine days.

With pre-orders opening April 13, D&D's first major release of 2026 is weeks away from its commercial moment of truth. Ravenloft: The Horrors Within — the anchor product of the newly launched "Season of Horror" (April–June) — represents WotC's most deliberate attempt yet to build sustained community momentum around a book release, wrapping a sourcebook inside a structured marketing season that includes organized play events, a Tarokka deck, a DM screen, and a five-map map pack, all coordinated for June 2026.

The book is overseen by longtime Ravenloft architect F. Wesley Schneider and delivers eight horror-themed subclasses, four new playable species (Dhampir, Hexblood, Lupin, and Reborn), new Dark Gift feats, and expanded Fear and Dread mechanics. Community buzz has already surfaced a notable data point: the book reportedly includes Cthulhu as a Darklord with 385 HP and a spell save DC of 25 — a CR-25-range stat block that signals WotC is reaching for genuine cosmic horror, not just Strahd retreads.

This marks the third Ravenloft sourcebook in 5e — a fact that is generating community fatigue in some corners even as gothic horror fans celebrate. The Season model itself is being watched closely across the industry as a structural bet: can packaging accessories, organized play, and social media cadence around a book's release window meaningfully grow engagement beyond launch week?

⬛ News in Brief
📜 Dark Sun Watch

All Signs Point to Athas for Q4

WotC has not officially announced the Season of Champions anchor title, but the evidence trail is substantial. A Psion class UA (May 2025), an Apocalyptic Subclasses UA featuring Preservation Druids, Gladiators, and Sorcerer-King Warlocks (August 2025), and a fresh USPTO trademark re-registration for Dark Sun (October 2025) collectively paint a picture that's hard to dismiss. The unconfirmed product thread on EN World currently sits at over 600 replies. Community consensus: it's coming. WotC is simply managing the hype sequencing around Ravenloft's launch window.

🎲 Third Party

Daggerheart Lands on Fantasy Grounds

Critical Role's Darrington Press expanded its VTT footprint this week with Daggerheart now available on Fantasy Grounds, complete with a dedicated ruleset supporting its signature Hope and Fear mechanics, integrated character sheets, automated adversary stat blocks, and illustrated card decks. This follows a Darrington Press player survey circulating this week soliciting feedback on play rates, digital tool preferences, and supplemental priorities — widely interpreted as groundwork for the upcoming Hope & Fear expansion Kickstarter, which will add four new classes, double the adversary and environment count, and introduce a GM screen.

📦 Premium Products

Beadle & Grimm's Silver Edition Enters Season of Horror

Third-party premium publisher Beadle & Grimm's confirmed this week that it will produce a Strahd's Silver Edition tie-in for Ravenloft: The Horrors Within, with pre-orders opening April 15 — two days after the standard book goes on sale. Full component details will be revealed at pre-order. Pricing is expected around $200 USD, consistent with previous Silver tier releases, though the company noted that tariff pressures and increased printing costs may affect final price. A Platinum Edition for Arcana Unleashed: Deadfall is also confirmed for summer pre-orders.

★ Editor's Analysis — Week of April 4, 2026

The next ten days are a commercial stress test for the new WotC. Pre-orders opening April 13 for Ravenloft: The Horrors Within will be the first real data signal on whether the 2024 ruleset has built the kind of enthusiastic consumer base that converts to immediate purchase behavior — or whether the "lukewarm" reception that industry observers have flagged is real and persistent.

The "Season" model is smart structurally: it gives retailers a calendar to plan around, it gives content creators a sustained content cadence to build coverage on, and it gives WotC a reason to stay in the conversation for three months rather than two weeks. But the model only works if the anchor product lands. Ravenloft is a known commodity — beloved setting, strong DM heritage, safe commercial bet. The question is whether safe is what the market wants right now, or whether the vocal "Release Dark Sun, you cowards" contingent represents a meaningful untapped demand signal.

Meanwhile, Darrington Press is building quietly but steadily. A VTT integration, an active player survey, and a known expansion campaign in the pipeline are the moves of a publisher playing a long game. The Critical Role machine gives Daggerheart an audience floor that no other D&D alternative has ever had. Whether that translates to persistent campaign play — the true measure of a TTRPG's health — remains the open question of 2026.
