Ravenloft pricing locked in — and Mythozoology just cleared $1M on day one

Confirmed
Ravenloft: The Horrors Within pre-orders open April 13 — $59.99, accessories at $24.99 each
WotC confirmed pricing and a tiered D&D Beyond release: Master Tier June 2, Hero Tier June 9, wide release June 16. Accessories include a Tarokka Deck, DM Screen, and Map Pack.
At full attach (book + all three accessories), a single customer spends $134.96 per season — this is the revenue thesis behind the Seasons model.
Founder's Read

The Tarokka Deck has genuine narrative utility and is the likeliest accessory to move. April 13 is the start of your content window — community search intent will spike and hold through June 16. If you're building horror content, the clock is running.

Mythozoology (DnD Shorts × Loot Tavern) hits $1M on day one of Kickstarter
The creator collab campaign for Heliana & Ryoko's Guide to Mythozoology raised over $1M on its first day. Campaign closes April 16. The project prominently labels itself 100% human-made.

This Week's Action

What this week tells your team — and what to do about it

For your content and products

The horror content window is real and the clock is running. Ravenloft pre-orders open April 13 and widest audience attention lands June 16 — a ten-week window where community search intent, forum discussion, and creator content concentrates on horror D&D. Anything you can produce in this space benefits from organic discovery lift that doesn't exist outside it.

The human-made positioning is a competitive signal, not just an ethical stance. Mythozoology leading with "100% human-made, guaranteed" is a purchase signal the audience actively responds to. For a founding team figuring out differentiation, this is low-hanging positioning fruit: lead with human craft, show the process, make authorship visible.

The gap in DM tools for horror is the clearest underserved space this week. WotC is releasing a sourcebook and atmospheric accessories — but no procedural tools for running horror well. Fear mechanics, tension pacing, gothic encounter design — a focused product ($10–15 PDF) addressing DM horror craft would land in a gap with active demand and no direct competition from WotC.

For your DM practice

The Tarokka Deck (June, $24.99) is worth pre-ordering if you're planning a Ravenloft or gothic horror campaign — it has genuine narrative utility beyond being a collectible. The DM Screen and Map Pack are lower priority unless you specifically need physical horror aids.

Multiple DM-advice creators are covering horror prep in the next 6–8 weeks. Sly Flourish and similar channels will produce practical prep guides — worth tracking as free research into what the DM audience finds most useful.

As players

Ravenloft pre-orders open April 13. The June 16 wide release will generate the most community build content, subclass guides, and setting material — worth waiting for if you're not a D&D Beyond subscriber.
